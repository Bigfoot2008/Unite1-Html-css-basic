THE IDEAL DIET FOR YOUR GOLDEN

In order for your dog to grow up as healthy as he is happy, it is important to choose the right dog food. Here are some useful tips. Food is, of course, a source of energy for dogs, but it is also - just as it is for humans - a real passion: it is good to follow some good advice on correct dog nutrition to ensure that dogs grow up both healthy and happy.


The age of the dog
and the amount of food

Each dog's age corresponds to a different nutritional need. This is why it is good to choose a dog food that is balanced specifically for your pet: puppy, junior, adult. So don't assume that food for us is also good for dogs!
The best dog food is one that has been created especially for dogs. Pay attention to the size of your dog's food: watch him carefully and lovingly, because if he gets too fat or too thin, you will need to review the size of his food.
The best way to start? Choose complete dog foods that are suitable for their age and build and always consult the nutritional tables described in them, which have been designed by expert nutritionists to provide the correct amounts of each ingredient your dog needs.

Dog food, which ones
and which ones not

The dog is a carnivorous animal: its first nutrient is animal protein, provided by fish and white meat, which gives it the energy to feed its muscles, run, jump and move. The second is fat: never underestimate its contribution, which is essential for storing energy.
Then there is fibre, which aids digestion: dog foods usually use maize flour, but mainly rice.
And finally, fatty acids such as Omega-3, powerful antioxidants, which help cells to grow and regenerate and combat ageing, followed by vitamins and minerals, which are essential for your dog's immune system to function properly.

Routines
are important!

Always feed your dog the recommended amounts at the same time and in the same place that is 'special' to him. Alongside the dog food, never forget fresh water, which should be changed often.
And if you wish, you can also create the habit of a small dog snack to be given after a meal: a small biscuit, a bone, a stick to chew can be used as a reward after a game, a good run or after performing an assigned task to perfection.
And it is one of the best ways of strengthening the wonderful relationship between you and him!

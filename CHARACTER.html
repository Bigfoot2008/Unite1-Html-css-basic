CHARACTER

The Golden Retriever is a dog with a very sweet and endearing appearance, but is also very popular for companionship due to its very sociable temperament. It was selected as a retrieval dog for objects in water and on land, for the hunting parties of the aristocrats. It is an obedient, intelligent dogw ith an innate willingness to work; gentle, friendly and confident.

CHARACTER AND TEMPERAMENT

The Breed Standard describes it as: “Obedient, intelligent and innately willing to work; gentle, friendly and trusting”. The Golden is deeply devoted to its relationship with its owner and dispenses affection to the whole family. As far as obedience is concerned, the Golden has a particular propensity to willingly carry out the requests of its human friend and hardly ever challenges his wishes as he is very dedicated and this leads him to interpret any command as work. He has a gentle and naive soul as he is trusting with everyone and approaches strangers, other dogs and animals well.

AFFECTIONATE TOWARDS HIS FAMILY

The Golden Retriever is a dog that adores its family of which it feels like a full member. It has excellent interaction with all members of the family and enjoys living in close contact with them. It is very obedient and likes to please its owner. As you can understand, it is a breed dedicated to its home and shares its days in perfect harmony with its members.

FRIENDLY WITH CHILDREN

The Golden Retriever is friendly with children. This is a talent that it cultivates from puppyhood. One of the Golden Retriever’s characteristics is that it can remain “puppy friendly” for many years while growing up. Its maturation, in fact, is completed at around 3 or 4 years of age. This is a great advantage when it comes to interacting with children. However, and in spite of the Golden’s wonderful character, interaction between children and dogs must necessarily take place under the watchful presence of an adult.

RELATIONSHIP WITH STRANGERS

As stated, the Golden is not a guard dog, so it does not have an aggressive instinct towards strangers. Obviously it will be more active and alert in the presence of strangers. It will alert if someone or something enters its territory but will never become determinedly aggressive.

SOCIALITY WITH OTHER DOGS

He is very sociable with other dogs and, if used from puppyhood, could be friendly with other animals. He is very playful and enjoys the slightest thing. He loves to play with other dogs and would be perfect if he could always have company.
